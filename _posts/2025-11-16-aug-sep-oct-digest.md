I've been bad with this recently. In my defense, I can only say that it's very time-and-energy-consuming to start an new general intelligence project at home :) and it has got me day-dreaming. However, here's a few things I've found interesting over the past 3 months:

- About the [oldest cheese](https://www.anthropology.net/p/the-oldest-cheese-3600-year-old-kefir).
- Did you know [Ciabatta is an 80's invention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ciabatta#Italy)? It's funny to think my parents are older than ciabatta.
- Misha Gromov's [writing on trees](https://www.ihes.fr/~gromov/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/trees-Cartie-1.pdf) and a particular tree, the [Porphyrian tree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porphyrian_tree) via the [history of set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_theory#Early_history).
- Relatedly, [anastomosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anastomosis), via [this interview](https://youtu.be/wawMjJUCMVw?si=oJ0p4uD-WlJdqtd-) with Warren McCulloch. Love his style.
- About [character analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Character_Analysis) and [physiognomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physiognomy).
- Two examples of RL in animals: [crows that were trained to pick up cigarette butts in Sweden](https://youtu.be/TklOxbWfRDM?si=E6gDAEZOqKokFgdw) (via Levin's [new paper on cognition](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-025-05319-6)) and [an octopus that was trained to play the piano](https://youtu.be/PcWnQ7fYzwI?si=dqW7aOhgiLSKXjvK)
